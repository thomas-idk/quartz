Correction 1, expanded: your Note on G.E. Moore says Moore 'relies on hinge propositions' and 'presupposes objective morality through certainty'. Chronologically it must run the other way: Principia Ethica (1903) predates On Certainty by half a century; what actually happened is that Wittgenstein took Moore's common-sense certainties (1925, 1939 papers) and re-described their epistemic status — they are not KNOWN (knowledge lives where doubt makes sense) but stand fast. If your framework tried to run modus ponens on hinges, it would instantly claim the kind of grounding the Munchhausen trilemma forbids, and collapse. The 'keep faith' relation (coherence with, non-betrayal of, articulation of) is weaker than entailment — and that weakness is what makes the framework honest. **Speaker notes:**

The three camps disagree about the METAPHYSICS of hinges (what kind of thing they are) while agreeing on their FUNCTION (rock-bottom enablers of rational evaluation).

Moyal-Sharrock: hinges cannot even be said, only shown in action — 'a certainty that is in no way propositional knowledge' — her phrase 'animal certainty' comes straight from OC 359: 'something animal'. Strength: closest to the text; kills the regress cleanly (nothing propositional left to justify). Cost: makes it hard to write hinges into arguments at all (you'll feel this cost when building — we quote hinge-SENTENCES while remembering they're really enacted).

Pritchard: hinges are commitments, not beliefs, so the 'universality of rational evaluation' thesis fails and skepticism never gets started. Coliva: the mid-position and your best construction material. Her 'extended rationality' says the demand 'justify your hinges!' is ill-formed because hinges fix what 'justify' means — rationality extends to its own enabling conditions. This is the professional version of 'stretching hinges into a logical framework'. Note her 2015 book literally addresses the epistemic status of basic LOGICAL laws — your T0.

Also: Wright's 'Warrant for Nothing (and Foundations for Free)?' (2004) — entitlement as non-evidential rational trust. And note there IS formal/logic-flavored work now (e.g. attempts at hinge operators in epistemic logic) — so 'can hinges be systematized?' has a live yes.

## Slide 5 — The Wall Every Foundation Hits — the Münchhausen Trilemma

**Slide text:**

THE WALL EVERY FOUNDATION HITS
the Münchhausen trilemma, met honestly

infinite regress
circularity
dogmatic stop
every reason needs a reason — the ladder never touches ground
the chain loops — A because B because A — the ground is painted on
you just stop somewhere — the ground is a decree
Hans Albert, Treatise on Critical Reason (1968) — every attempted justification of anything ends on one of three horns. He is right about justification.

the hinge answer — a fourth relation to the ground
The regress ends — but not in a proposition asserted without proof. It ends where propositions run out: in a practice lived. “Giving grounds comes to an end — the end is not certain propositions striking us as true; it is our acting.” (§204)  Dogma is a defended assertion inside the game. A hinge asserts nothing — so it cannot be a dogmatic assertion. Coliva's upgrade: accepting hinges is constitutive of what “rational” means, so demanding their justification is ill-formed, like demanding the meter-stick be measured. Apel's cousin move: deny the presuppositions of argument while arguing and you refute yourself performatively.

Albert's rejoinder — and the honest verdict
“You have re-labeled the stop, not removed it.” Fair — as an answer to the demand for God's-eye grounding, hinges offer none. Verdict: the trilemma is dissolved for the practice of justifying, undefeated for absolute grounding. The framework we build will claim the first and never the second — that is what “as flawless as possible” means here.

**Speaker notes:**

The trilemma is Albert's modern sharpening of Agrippa's five modes (Sextus Empiricus). Note the lineage: Popper's fallibilism -> Albert's critical rationalism. Albert deployed it against ALL foundationalisms, including Apel's.

Why a hinge is not the third horn: the third horn is an ASSERTION exempted from defense by fiat — a move within the justification game that cheats. A hinge is not a move in the game at all (Moyal-Sharrock: not propositional; Coliva: not warrantable in principle; Pritchard: not a belief). 'Nothing is asserted, so nothing is dogmatically asserted.' The stop is principled (constitutive), not arbitrary (decreed): you can show FOR EACH hinge how doubting it destroys the practice of doubting (OC 115: 'the game of doubting itself presupposes certainty').

Apel vs Albert is the German 1970s version of this exact fight (the 'Begrundungsstreit'): Apel's transcendental pragmatics says the trilemma only bites if you restrict yourself to semantic-deductive justification — move to the PRAGMATIC level (what every arguer must presuppose to argue at all: truth-orientation, equal standing of interlocutors, non-coercion) and you find 'ultimate grounding' via performative non-deniability. Albert's reply (consistently): that's just horn three in pragmatic costume; presuppositions of OUR practice bind only those who play. Keep both voices — your framework sides with Apel's MECHANISM (performative non-deniability) while conceding Albert's POINT (no view-from-nowhere). That concession is not a weakness; it is the honesty that makes the rest credible. Munchhausen pulled himself out of the swamp by his own hair; we don't claim to leave the swamp — we claim the swamp has a solid bottom exactly where we stand, because standing is what 'solid' means.

T2 is where your framework departs from pure Wittgenstein exegesis and starts building — flag that honestly. On Certainty itself stays epistemological; but its deepest remark (204: acting at the bottom) points here: if the bottom of the language-game is ACTION, then the bottom is already norm-laden, because action (unlike mere movement) is done under descriptions, aimed, correctable, criticizable. It has full hinge phenomenology: never learned, never checked, doubt-destroying. This matters: the framework's moral core will inherit hinge-grade inescapability exactly to the extent that it draws on T2 alone plus consistency.

**Slide text:**

Hume's guillotine — and three ways to flank it

No ought from is, by derivation. (Treatise 3.1.1) Valid and unbeaten: if the premises contain no “ought,” no valid inference puts one in the conclusion. The framework concedes this completely — and goes around.

1 · parity — flank it
2 · constitutive — bridge it
3 · practice-internal oughts
At bedrock NOTHING is derived: “the world exists” is exactly as unproven as “gratuitous cruelty is wrong.” The gap survives, but stops being an objection to morality in particular — morality is merely like everything else. The guillotine cuts derivations; hinges were never derivations.
Gewirth's PGC: I act → I need freedom & well-being → I must claim them as my due → consistency forces me to grant them to every agent. The ought isn't derived from is — it's excavated from what acting already commits you to.
Searle: “he promised” already contains “he ought.” Foot & Anscombe: inside a form of life, goodness is as factual as health. Wittgenstein-native — but inherits the relativity of forms of life.

solid · modest

strong — if slide 10 survives

native · relativism-prone
the framework uses all three: parity clears the ground · the constitutive bridge carries the core · practice-oughts furnish the rooms

**Speaker notes:**

Hume's exact text (Treatise 3.1.1.27) is an observation about authors sliding from 'is' to 'ought' without explanation — the logical point (no ought-conclusions from ought-free premises in a valid inference) is its standard sharpening. Concede it: it is a theorem, effectively. Every 'refutation' either smuggles a normative premise or redefines 'ought'.

Flank 1 (parity) is the distinctive HINGE contribution to this old fight, and it is stronger than it looks: the usual force of 'you can't derive ought from is' assumes the IS-side stands on something firmer. On Certainty removes that assumption — the is-side also floats on underived certainties. So the meta-ethical situation is symmetric: both domains bottom out in what cannot be justified but cannot be lived without. Mackie's 'queerness' objection (Ethics, 1977: objective values would be metaphysically weird) also softens under parity: moral hinges are no queerer than logical ones — neither is a spooky OBJECT; both are grammar of practice.

Flank 2 (Gewirth): the dialectically necessary method starts INSIDE the agent's first-person standpoint ('I do X for purpose E') and extracts only what that standpoint cannot deny. The universalization step (from 'my agency needs protecting' to 'agency as such does') is the strongest link; critics (including the famous Ethics 1985 exchange) attack the move from 'I need freedom' to 'I have a RIGHT to freedom'. Beyleveld's defense (The Dialectical Necessity of Morality, 1991) is the deepest available.

Flank 3: Searle 'How to Derive Ought from Is' (1964) works only inside the institution of promising — which is fine for us: institutions are local hinge-structures. Foot's Natural Goodness (2001) and Anscombe's 'Modern Moral Philosophy' (1958) ground oughts in species-form — closest to forms-of-life talk. Its relativism worry is handled at T3 by the universality test (slide 11) and the exemption criterion (slide 12).

**Speaker notes:**

Enoch, 'Agency, Shmagency: Why Normativity Won't Come from What Is Constitutive of Action' (Philosophical Review, 2006) — THE modern objection to the constitutive bridge. His deeper point: from inside chess, constitutive rules bind; but nothing makes you play chess — so constitutive norms are conditional (IF you play), and morality was supposed to be unconditional.

The hinge reply reframes what kind of thing agency is: chess is optional practice; agency is hinge — there is no standing-place outside it from which the option could be exercised. Sleep, catatonia, death end agency but are not EXITS TAKEN (taking is acting). Ferrero's 'Constitutivism and the Inescapability of Agency' (Oxford Studies in Metaethics 4, 2009) makes this move within the mainstream literature; your framework strengthens it by giving 'inescapable' a worked-out epistemology (hinge status, OC 115's branch-sawing structure) rather than leaving it a metaphor.

Enoch's counter-counter ('inescapability still isn't normativity — a prison isn't a reason') deserves respect: hence the honest cost line. The framework's response is to LOWER THE PRIZE: it never promised bindingness-from-nowhere (slide 5 already conceded no view-from-nowhere exists for ANYTHING). Binding-for-every-possible-arguer is the maximum coherent prize, and the constitutive bridge wins exactly that. Anyone who shows up to dispute it has already paid the entry fee (Apel's performative point). The never-arguing amoralist pays nothing — and receives nothing: no standing to complain when contained. This is not a bug; it is what Hobbes (your shelf) was about.

Pleasants' key papers: 'Wittgenstein and Basic Moral Certainty' (Philosophia, 2008), 'Wittgenstein, Ethics and Basic Moral Certainty' (Inquiry, 2009), and his work on moral epistemology and the wrongness of killing. ## Slide 12 — Obstacle №3 — The Riverbed Problem

**Slide text:**

OBSTACLE Nº3 · THE HARDEST ONE
the riverbed problem — slavery was lived hinge-tight

“The river-bed of thoughts may shift.” (§97)  Whole civilizations held slavery's permissibility with hinge-grade certainty, for millennia. So is “universal moral hinge” just “prejudice not yet eroded”? If moral bedrock shifts, doesn't the whole T3 floor float?

the exemption criterion — the answer, and it has teeth
A real T3 hinge cannot be denied for SOME humans while claimed for others without special pleading.
Slavery always needed an exemption story — “natural slaves” (Aristotle's worst page), curse of Ham, race science. But exemption stories are ARGUMENTS: inside the game, addressable, attackable. That is precisely why abolitionist arguments could work: deeper hinges (T2 — the slave manifestly acts, aims, responds to reasons) ground down the local crust. The certainty was never bedrock — it was crust doing a bedrock impression, held up by an argument pretending not to be one.
Moral progress, redefined: consistency pressure from deeper tiers eroding local hinge-crust.

it predicts, not just consoles: wherever a practice leans on an exemption story today, expect erosion. Run the test yourself: factory farming? borders? future generations? the criterion tells you where to dig.
and it humbles: our own floor carries crust too. The framework cannot promise timeless content — only a method for telling bedrock from crust, and the honesty to keep testing.

**Speaker notes:**

This slide answers the strongest objection to ANY hinge-based ethics — the historical-relativist one. Pleasants himself wrote on this ('The Structure of Moral Revolutions', on slavery and moral change): his line is that basic moral certainty attaches to the WRONGNESS OF SUFFERING-INFLICTION as such, while its SCOPE (who counts) has been the historically variable crust. That scope/core distinction is exactly what the exemption criterion formalizes.

Why the criterion is principled rather than ad hoc: it falls straight out of T3's universality test (slide 11). A certainty needed for justification-practice-as-such must extend to every party the practice addresses; restricting it therefore requires an ARGUMENT for the restriction (the exemption story) — and the moment the restriction rests on argument, it has left the riverbed and become an ordinary, attackable move in the game. Bedrock needs no story; crust always has one. 'Natural slaves': Politics I.5 — and notice Aristotle ARGUING for it, which by the criterion already gives the game away.

Abolition case study: Frederick Douglass' 'What to the Slave is the Fourth of July?' works exactly this way — he does not argue slavery is wrong from first principles; he exhibits the master's own hinges (the slave is addressed by law, held responsible, evangelized — treated as an agent everywhere except where it costs) and lets consistency pressure do the demolition.

The two bottom panels are the framework's ethics-of-belief: the criterion is a RESEARCH TOOL (test current practices for exemption stories) and a HUMILITY ENGINE (assume our crust exists; hunt it). This converts the riverbed problem from a refutation into the framework's growth mechanism.

**Speaker notes:**

How T4 relates to the classic theories — it is a GROUNDING story that then licenses a broadly deontic core with a virtue-ethical delivery system: (a) vs. utilitarianism: welfare matters (harm-aversion is T3) but aggregation that erases standing violates the core — you may not dissolve persons into a sum (each is a hinge-holder, not a utility container); (b) vs. Kantian deontology: the core IS recognizably Kantian (universalizability = the exemption criterion; justifiability-to-each = kingdom of ends), but grounded in enacted hinges rather than transcendental freedom — sturdier footing, humbler claims; (c) vs. virtue ethics proper: Aristotle supplies moral psychology (NE II: hexis by habituation — nobody reasons themselves into courage) while T0-T3 supply what virtue must keep faith WITH, solving virtue ethics' notorious grounding circularity (the virtuous person is the standard... of what makes a person virtuous).

On 'binding for anyone who lives a human life': this is Coliva's extended-rationality move transplanted to ethics — moral rationality EXTENDS to the hinges that make moral practice possible. The amoralist of slide 10 remains logically possible and existentially rare; the framework's answer to him was never going to be an argument (nobody's is — that's the parity point); it is containment plus the standing offer of re-entry.

The Episteme paper 'Deep Disagreement, Hinge Commitments, and Intellectual Humility' completes the kit: recognizing one's own commitments as hinges (unproven, inherited, filtering) is the epistemically honest posture and the politically stabilizing one.

Why underdetermination is the honest verdict: the universal floor (T0-T2, thin T3) is COMMON to all parties by definition of 'universal' — so it cannot by itself select among them; selection requires local hinges + empirical bets, which is where legitimate politics lives. The framework thus converges, from a different direction, with Rawls' insight about reasonable pluralism (Political Liberalism: no comprehensive doctrine wins a free society outright; overlapping consensus is the prize) — your version might be called overlapping HINGES: parties disagree at the top floors while standing on one floor at T0-T2.

Exclusion zone: note it does real work despite its thinness — Nazi race law, chattel slavery, caste, gulag: each rests on an explicit who-doesn't-count story, i.e., institutionalized special pleading, i.e., a violation visible from INSIDE any justification practice (slide 12's criterion). The deck deliberately does not pick for you — picking IS the political act, made with open eyes about which of your commitments are local.

Marx note: 'ruling ideas of each age are the ideas of its ruling class' (German Ideology / Manifesto) — hinge-translation: power works by promoting its local hinges to unquestionable status; critique de-naturalizes them. The hinge version is more even-handed than Marx's: it cuts against every establishment, including revolutionary ones the morning after they win.
