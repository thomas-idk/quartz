# Personal reflections on Hinge Propositions

**📌 Remember to read: Danièle Moyal-Sharrock's taxonomy of hinges.**

## The question I kept circling

Could you actually build a logical framework — a set of axioms — out of hinge propositions themselves?

The thought started from something almost childishly simple: nobody can live as if a door is fully open and fully closed at the same time. Not "won't" — *can't*. It's not a belief you hold, it's a wall you hit before belief even starts. That felt like a clue. If some hinges are *that* basic, shared by literally anyone who has ever stood in front of a door, then maybe hinges aren't all equally personal. Some might be near-universal. Others — like "my name is Thomas" — obviously aren't; they're mine alone.

So the idea: sort hinges into two piles.
- **Folder one** — the broadly shared hinges, the ones nearly every human stands on without exception.
- **Folder two** — everything else: the personal, the local, the culturally specific.

Put the weight of the work into folder one. If enough people already stand on the same unquestioned ground, maybe *that's* where a shared logical foundation could actually be built — and everything else reasoned outward from there.

## Turns out this already has a name

I ran this past another AI chat, expecting either a dead end or a slog to formalize from scratch. Instead: this is close to exactly Danièle Moyal-Sharrock's actual taxonomy of hinges. She splits them into four kinds, not two, but mine map cleanly onto hers:

| Her category | What it means | Where mine lands |
|---|---|---|
| **Universal** | Shared by all human beings — objects persist unobserved, the earth is old, other people have inner lives | → my "folder one" |
| **Local** | True for a community or culture, not everyone | → my "folder two" |
| **Personal** | "My name is X," "I have two hands" | → my "folder two" |
| **Linguistic** | The certainty baked into words having stable meaning at all | *(didn't even think of this one — it's the hinge underneath the other hinges)* |

So the sorting instinct wasn't naive — it was basically right, just not yet fine-grained. The part I hadn't considered is that even *language meaning something* is itself a hinge, sitting under all three others. You can't doubt whether "hand" means what it usually means and still ask a coherent question about your hand.

## What's still open

The real test of my original idea — build from the universal folder outward, reach conclusions — is exactly what I haven't done yet. Moyal-Sharrock apparently already worked through some of this rigorously. Before trying to build my own framework from scratch, read her first and see how far she actually took it, and where she thinks it breaks down.

*Open question to sit with: if the universal hinges are the ones nobody can coherently doubt, is a "logical foundation" built from them actually doing any work — or is it just restating that doubt has to stop somewhere, dressed up as a system?*
